\section{Conclusion}

We have shown that the Cube Rule of Food can be lifted from internet meme to rigorous geometry: edible artifacts map to coordinates $(i,j,k)$ in a third-order tensor $T$, with cube morphology $i$, protein taxonomy $j$, and starch substrate $k$ as the axes of a discrete culinary state space.
Canonical Cube Rule examples occupy well-defined cells; empty cells correspond to conceivable-but-unobserved dishes; and collisions---multiple foods at the same $(i,j,k)$---become topological facts rather than matters of taste.
By constraining an LLM to propose candidates under strict axis matching, lexical guardrails, and a review gate, we have extended the support set $\mathcal{S}$ beyond the canonical seed while keeping the tensor falsifiable and the Rule's edge cases explicit: salad cannot contain starch; the rice exception applies to plain rice as a mono-food; and the \term{horseshoe theory of salad} captures the gradient from single-component salad (all lettuce or all croutons) to nachos (lettuce and croutons together).

This formalism is not a dead end---it is a scaffold.
The index sets $I$, $J$, and $K$ can grow in principled ways.
The cube axis $i$ may gain a dedicated \emph{single-starch} category, resolving the Rule's ``interpret as you wish'' for plain rice and bowls of croutons without collapsing them into salad.
The protein axis $j$ may admit gelatin (and thus jello, soup dumplings, and other gelatin-bound dishes) as a distinct type, opening a sparse but vivid region of $\mathcal{S}$.
The starch axis $k$ may be extended to include sugar-dominant substrates---candy, nougat, Snickers bars---so that Snickers salad and its ilk cease to be boundary cases and take their place at a named coordinate.
Each expansion refines the atlas: the menu remains coordinate-bearing, and the space of conceivable foods becomes ever more navigable.

What this research shows is that morphology, protein, and starch can be made to carry weight not as loose labels but as indices.
The future of the tensor is not a checklist of next steps but a widening of the same idea: that cuisine can be charted, that empty cells can be filled with discipline, and that the Cube Rule---with its salad prohibition, its rice exception, and its horseshoe of lettuce and croutons---deserves a formalism that is both strict and expandable.

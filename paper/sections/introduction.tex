\section{Introduction}

This project studies a formal extension of the Cube Rule of Food using a rank-3 tensor $T$ over three categorical axes.
We work with two \emph{theoretical} state spaces: $\RealFoods$ (foods taken to have been cooked in recorded history---a set we approximate, not observe in full) and $\ConceivableFoods$ (all conceivable foods, including in principle those not yet or never prepared---a space we cannot directly verify), with $\RealFoods \subseteq \ConceivableFoods$.
The immediate objective is to define a compact ontology in which each edible artifact in $\ConceivableFoods$ is assigned a coordinate triple $(i,j,k)$ in the discrete latent space $\{1,\ldots,I\} \times \{1,\ldots,J\} \times \{1,\ldots,K\}$, so that canonical Cube Rule examples are representable and ambiguous cases are explicit.
Our framing treats occupancy, sparsity, and ambiguity as geometric properties of $T$ rather than anecdotal judgments.
In that sense, the menu is not merely descriptive but \emph{coordinate-bearing}: cuisine is rendered less as a list and more as a chart atlas, albeit one with better snacks.

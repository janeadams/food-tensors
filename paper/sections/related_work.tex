\section{Related Work}

The Cube Rule is positioned here as prior edible ontology work and is connected to two external strands of literature.
The first strand is modern LLM control and prompting: \term{prompt taxonomies}, \term{schema-constrained generation}, \term{grammar-constrained decoding}, \term{guided decoding}, and \term{retrieval-augmented generation} provide language for how structured candidate rows are elicited and validated for assignment to cells $(i,j,k)$ of $T$.
The second strand is tensor and geometry-oriented representation theory: \term{tensor completion}, \term{manifold hypothesis}, \term{simplicial complex} constructions, \term{persistent homology}, and \term{topological data analysis} provide language for discussing the support set $\mathcal{S} = \{(i,j,k) : T_{i,j,k}=1\}$, sparsity patterns, and collision structure in higher-order categorical spaces.
Classical combinatorics references are also relevant for occupancy guarantees, including direct pigeonhole-style collision arguments when the number of foods exceeds $|I \times J \times K| = IJK$.
Taken together, these literatures supply both procedural rigor and just enough conceptual garnish to let a food taxonomy sound almost alarmingly formal.

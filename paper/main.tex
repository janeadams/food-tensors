\title{A Tensor-Based Expansion of the Cube Rule for Generative Nutrient Morphology}
\author{Dr. Jane Lydia Adams, Ph.D.}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We formalize the Cube Rule of Food as a third-order tensor $T \in \{0,1\}^{I \times J \times K}$ whose axes encode cube morphology, protein type, and starch type.
Within this ontology, canonical foods can be placed reproducibly, collisions become explicit coordinates with multiple occupants, and empty cells can be interpreted as constrained sites for culinary speculation.
We show in particular that salad occupies exactly the \texttt{starch\_type=none} slice, examine mono-starch edge cases such as plain rice and bowls of croutons, and characterize sparsity in the tensor as a mix of structural impossibility and uneaten potential.
To populate and analyze the space, we combine ontology-constrained LLM generation, deterministic post-generation filtering, occupancy visualizations, and a supporting software pipeline that renders both the paper figures and an interactive local web viewer for the tensor outputs.
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}
We introduce a tensor-based formulation of the Cube Rule of Food in which dishes are embedded in a third-order tensor $T \in \{0,1\}^{I \times J \times K}$ spanning cube morphology (index $i$), protein taxonomy ($j$), and starch substrate ($k$).
This formulation supports reproducible placement of canonical Cube Rule foods, detection of collisions among distinct dishes at the same cell, and constrained generation of candidate dishes for vacant cells $(i,j,k)$ with $T_{i,j,k}=0$. This induces a weak topology of taste on the index lattice.
To operationalize the framework, we use a constrained generative procedure that proposes candidate dishes for empty cells, enforces axis-consistency constraints on $i$, $j$, and $k$, and filters the results before downstream review.
\end{abstract}
